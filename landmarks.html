The Gateway of India, located in Mumbai, is an iconic arch monument built during the 20th century to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

The Lotus Temple, in New Delhi, is a template that is open to all faiths and religions, symbolizing the peace and unity of life.

India is home to numerous iconic landmarks that reflect its rich history, culture, and architecture.

Some of the most famous landmarks include the romantic Taj Mahal, the beautifully built Golden Temple in Amristar, the Indian Gate, and the peacefully abiding Lotus Temple. Each of these landmarks symbolize a greater meaning that drives our world for the better, often times at peace.

These landmarks not only attract millions of tourists from around the world but also serve as important cultural and historical symbols for the people of India.

Visiting these landmarks offers a glimpse into India's diverse heritage and the architectural brilliance of different eras! Take a look at the images on the right and left to gain a sense of what India has to offer its visitors.

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

The Golden Temple, in Amritsar, is a sacred Sikh shrine known for its golden dome and spiritual significance.
